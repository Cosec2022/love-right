# Catalogue gap: Story 13

## What the first twelve already favour

The catalogue repeatedly uses a two-person urban encounter: beach or transit coincidence (01, 02, 07), recovered history around a wedding or an almost-wedding (03, 08), and a relationship asked to prove itself at a private threshold (09–12). Several leads are emotionally articulate, patient men whose restraint is itself the reward. Conflict often comes from old love, a family invitation, a private room, or the fear that intimacy will cost independence. The Story 08 rewrite review also identifies a recurrent failure mode: refusal is cosmetically offered but the plot carries the player into the same place anyway.

## Space worth adding

The catalogue has little about making something public with other people who have equal claims: a community workplace, shared authorship, professional credit, and the difference between being loved privately and being backed publicly. It has no story built around rehearsal, collective cultural work, or a love interest who is charismatic precisely because he is ambitious and difficult—not because he is endlessly tactful.

## Three candidate concepts

| Concept | Hook and reality | Desire / cost | Difference and risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Last rehearsal** | A community theatre loses its hall in nine days. Its stage manager discovers the actor she is falling for has taken a touring offer that uses the troupe's unfinished work. | She wants a future that is hers, not a sentimental rescue; he wants paid work and authorship. Transparency can cost both jobs and the troupe's trust. | Adds public work and collective stakes. Risk: theatre jargon or a love interest made too villainous. |
| **The night market lease** | A tenant representative and a ceramic seller must decide whether to renew a night market after a fire-safety order. | She wants financial security; he wants to keep a craft community alive. | Adds small-business reality. Risk: could become a generic “save the place” plot. |
| **The jury room at the food festival** | A food writer is asked to judge her neighbourhood cook's application while a sponsor controls the rules. | She wants professional credibility; he wants access to a career-changing platform. | Adds ethics and public taste. Risk: professional imbalance can make romance feel coercive. |

## Selected concept

**Story 13: 《最后一场排练，他没有上台》** is selected. Its attraction grows through concrete collaboration, while both people retain claims that cannot be solved by a single confession. The player must decide what public loyalty, fair credit, and private desire can coexist. It avoids old-love reunion, wedding pressure, domestic rescue, and a therapist-like male lead. The main craft risk—turning a collective into scenery—is handled by giving the troupe members authority, schedules, and vetoes.
