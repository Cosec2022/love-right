# Story 13 brief (frozen v1)

## Story promise

Over nine pressured days in a closing community theatre, the player experiences the rare intimacy of being trusted in public: not being “chosen” in secret, but deciding whether a person who wants her can also stand beside her when credit, rent, and other people's work are at stake. Love is possible only at the cost of refusing an easy career-saving deal or accepting a future with an honest distance.

## Protagonist

**乔安**, 31, woman, player viewpoint; the theatre's salaried stage manager and the only person with the keys, budget spreadsheet, and fire-safety log. Her landlord has given the collective nine days to clear the warehouse hall. She wants the production to finish cleanly and has an offer to become operations manager at a touring company in Suzhou. She fears becoming indispensable to people who never ask what it costs her. Her flaw is deciding alone, then calling the decision “efficiency.” She can hurt people by withholding a plan until it is too late to answer it. She must decide whether to sign any licence, accept the Suzhou job, and tell the collective the truth; no romantic partner can decide that for her.

## Love interest

**陆惟**, 34, actor and co-writer of the unfinished play. He has a volatile comic timing, dislikes careful speeches, and tries to turn shame into a joke before anyone can see it. He wants a paid national tour because he has spent years working unpaid in community theatre and is exhausted by precariousness. He approaches 安 because she is the only person who says plainly what a scene cannot carry. He has accepted a producer's exploratory offer to tour a revised version before asking the collective; he avoids saying so because he knows it is unfair. His defect is treating urgency as permission. He can hurt the player by making her solve the consequences of his ambition. His independent goal is paid acting work; he may leave the relationship and the city.

## Supporting characters

## Core conflict and reality constraints

The troupe's last performance is in nine days; a producer offers a paid tour only if the script is delivered in four days and credits can be cleared. The lease ends at midnight after the final show; the hall's power test is Tuesday, and no one may use the space after the inspector seals it. A licence requires 唐瑛 and both credited co-writers. 安 knows the venue accounts and the Suzhou offer; 陆惟 knows the producer wants a smaller, marketable version; 吴照 knows he was not copied on the initial email. Nobody may invent consent for another person. Buses stop at 23:20, the nearby print shop closes at 20:00, and an unsigned licence cannot be presented as a troupe decision.

Both desire each other and a future in theatre. Both cannot casually concede: 安 cannot turn shared labour into a private favour; 陆惟 cannot survive another year of unpaid work. The moral cost is public credit and the choir's use of a disappearing space.

## Relationship temperature (0–6)

0 guarded colleagues; 1 notices how the other works; 2 accepts a one-to-one task; 3 shares an inconvenient truth; 4 names attraction with a boundary; 5 chooses a relationship form in light of career facts; 6 bears the public consequence together. Every increase needs an observable rehearsal, disclosure, repair, or public action.

## Prohibited shortcuts

- 陆惟 is never an always-correct counsellor.
- No character recites a product philosophy of love.
- One late conversation cannot settle credit, rent, and desire.
- A refusal is not treated as continuing permission.
- A kiss is not a relationship contract.
- The last answer cannot overwrite signed documents or earlier disclosure.
- Supporting characters do not make way for the couple.
- A normal question is not delayed merely to manufacture misunderstanding.
- Narration does not diagnose the player for her.
